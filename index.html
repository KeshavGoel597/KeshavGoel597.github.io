About Me

I’m a first-year BTech student with a burning passion for technology and a curiosity that drives me to explore the world of coding. My journey began in my first semester when I started learning C and C++, captivated by how lines of code could solve complex problems. From writing my first "Hello, World!" program to building small console applications, I’ve been hooked on the thrill of creating something functional and meaningful from scratch.

While I’m still early in my tech adventure, I’m diving into web development with enthusiasm, experimenting with HTML, CSS, and the basics of JavaScript. This website is one of my first steps—a personal project to showcase my growing skills and learn by doing. I love the idea of crafting websites that are not only useful but also visually appealing, and I’m excited to keep exploring tools and techniques to bring my ideas to life.

Outside of coding, I’m fascinated by a wide range of interests that keep me inspired. Whether it’s solving math puzzles, reading about the latest advancements in AI, or spending hours tweaking my code to make it just right, I’m always chasing new challenges. I also enjoy stepping away from the screen to immerse myself in music, sketch designs for fun projects, or simply daydream about the next big idea I want to tackle.

What motivates me is the belief that technology can make a difference, no matter how small. As a beginner, I’m eager to learn from every experience—be it a bug I debug or a new concept I grasp. My goal is to grow into a developer who builds solutions that connect people and spark joy, one project at a time. For now, I’m soaking up knowledge, asking questions, and enjoying the journey of discovery in this ever-evolving field.

I’m thrilled to be at the start of this path, with so much to learn and explore. Whether it’s mastering a new programming concept or sharing what I’ve learned with others, I’m all in for creating, experimenting, and growing as a tech enthusiast. Here’s to turning my curiosity into creations that leave a mark!
